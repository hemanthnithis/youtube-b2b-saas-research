## 📊 The Two Axes

```
X-Axis → Narrative Strength   (based on 24 Narrative Principles)
Y-Axis → Persuasiveness        (based on 3 Rhetorical Principles)
```

Because narratives can be broken down into individual principles, you can look across an entire corporate communication strategy and ask surgical questions.

---

## 🔍 Auditing Narrative Strength

- Do they consistently communicate the customer's identity transformation?
- Is the primary conflict that prevents that from happening clear?
- How well do they show what's at stake?

---

## 🎯 Auditing Persuasiveness

- How are they appealing to logic?
- How do they appeal to emotion?
- How do they communicate authority?

---

## 🌐 Auditing One Company vs. Multiple Companies

- Audit **one company** across multiple touchpoints, and you can start to see the holes that might give a prospect pause.
- Audit **multiple companies**, and you expose narrative gaps in the market that can be exploited.

---

## 💡 Example: The Narrative Gap

Competitors A and B are fairly persuasive. They use statistics to support their claims, their pricing makes sense for the offer, and they have testimonials from real people.

But the way they speak about the problem is nearly identical, and practically clinical.

They might say:

> *"80% of customers experience [problem]"*

But what nobody does is say:

> *"This is how [problem] had them fighting off tears as they struggled to solve it"*

This is the kind of analysis that helps you identify the very small, very specific ways you can ✨differentiate✨ that have an outsized compounding effect.

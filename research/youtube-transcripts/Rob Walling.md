## 📖 Executive Overview

Most founders approach content marketing without a framework — and waste thousands of dollars producing content that never converts. This framework solves that problem by applying **Eugene Schwartz's Five Stages of Customer Awareness** (a concept from the 1960s) to modern B2B SaaS content marketing and SEO strategy.

The core insight: **not every piece of content is equal**, and the stage your prospect is in determines what content will move them forward. A blog post titled *"7 Email Marketing Myths"* serves a radically different audience than *"MailChimp vs. ActiveCampaign: A Direct Comparison."* Both have a place — but only when deliberately mapped to the right stage of the funnel.

> **Origin Story:** This framework was born at a Tiny Seed kickoff event in London, when a founder panicked after being given 15 blog post ideas with no way to prioritize them. The answer was to take a 60-year-old advertising concept and apply it as a practical B2B content prioritization system.

**The single most important rule:** *Start at the bottom.* Build your most-aware (Stage 5) content first, because it serves double duty — it closes sales AND drives organic traffic from high-intent buyers. Then work your way up the funnel.

## 🧠 The Five Stages of Customer Awareness

Eugene Schwartz identified five distinct stages that every buyer moves through before making a purchase. Understanding where your prospect sits determines everything: what they search for, what content resonates, and how close they are to converting.

```
Stage 1 → Unaware
Stage 2 → Problem Aware
Stage 3 → Solution Aware
Stage 4 → Product Aware
Stage 5 → Most Aware
```

---

### 🔴 Stage 1 — Unaware

**Definition:** Your prospect doesn't realize they have a problem. They have no idea there is a way to improve their current situation.

**B2B Reality Check:** This is the hardest and most expensive stage to market in. If people don't know they have a problem, they aren't Googling it, they aren't in forums talking about it, and no amount of inbound content will find them — you must go to them.

**Example (Realtor → Email Marketing):** A realtor who doesn't know they need more leads yet. They have no awareness that email marketing could solve their problem.

**Tactics:**
- Cold outbound (email, LinkedIn)
- High-level YouTube tutorials (*"Email Marketing Full Course"*)
- Broad educational content that introduces the category

> [!CAUTION]
> **Avoid spending significant resources here early.** If you are an early-stage SaaS company, unaware prospects have the longest path to conversion. Focus your energy on Stages 2–5 first.

---

### 🟠 Stage 2 — Problem Aware

**Definition:** Your prospect knows they have a problem. They are actively searching for answers but are not yet familiar with solutions or categories that could help them.

**What they search for:** *"How do I send bulk email?" / "How do I find more real estate leads?" / "What is email marketing?"*

**Example (Realtor):** The realtor now knows they need more leads and starts Googling broadly — but they don't yet know "email marketing software" is a thing. They're still at *"I have a problem, what do I do?"*

**Tactics:**
- Templates (e.g., contract templates, workshop templates)
- How-to guides for the problem itself — not your product
- Broad educational content that solves the immediate pain point

**Real-World B2B Examples:**
- **Sewell (Electronic Signatures):** Ranks for contract and agreement templates — giving away the thing the customer needs *right now* before they know e-signature software exists. Pulls them into the funnel naturally.
- **SessionLab (Workshop Planning):** Publishes content like *"How to Become a Great Workshop Facilitator"* — high-level content for people who don't yet know workshop agenda software is a solution.
- **Veed.io (Video Editing):** SEOs aggressively for tools-based queries like *"add subtitles to video"* — capturing problem-aware users before they know Veed exists.
- **UserList (SaaS Onboarding):** Publishes *"What is User Onboarding: The Ultimate Guide for SaaS Companies"* — top-of-funnel education that builds trust before a product pitch.

---

### 🟡 Stage 3 — Solution Aware

**Definition:** Your prospect knows that solutions like yours exist. They understand the category but haven't yet compared specific products.

**What they search for:** *"Best email marketing software" / "What is marketing automation?" / "Email marketing tools compared"*

**Example (Realtor):** The realtor now understands that email marketing platforms exist and starts browsing Capterra, G2, Reddit, and Quora for recommendations.

**Key Channels at This Stage:**
- **Capterra & G2** — Prospects who land here are already at Stage 3 or 4. Advertising on Capterra can yield high conversion rates due to the intent of the audience.
- **Reddit & Quora** — Buyers at this stage frequently post in forums asking for recommendations.
- **YouTube** — *"Best [category] software"* review videos perform well here.

**Tactics:**
- Get listed and reviewed on Capterra, G2, SourceForge
- Monitor Reddit, Hacker News, Indie Hackers for category discussions
- Publish category-level comparison and educational content

> [!TIP]
> Search your category in incognito mode (e.g., *"best electronic signature software"*) to see which review platform ranks highest — Capterra, G2, or SourceForge — and prioritize your presence there first.

---

### 🔵 Stage 4 — Product Aware

**Definition:** Your prospect knows your product exists. They are actively comparing you to competitors and deciding if you are the right fit.

**What they search for:** *"MailChimp vs. ActiveCampaign" / "Drip alternative" / "Clavio pricing vs. MailChimp"*

**Example (Realtor):** The realtor is now comparing MailChimp to Klaviyo to ActiveCampaign. They are deep in evaluation mode.

**Tactics:**
- **Versus Pages** — *"[Your Product] vs. [Competitor]"* — only effective once your brand has enough search volume to warrant it.
- **Alternative-To Pages** — *"Best [Competitor] Alternative"* — works even in early stages by piggybacking on a competitor's existing brand authority.
- **Case Studies** — Social proof for prospects actively evaluating you.
- **Competitor Mention Monitoring** — Use tools (see Pillar 3) to track when people mention your product by name in forums; those are Stage 4 buyers actively looking for input.

**Real-World Example:**
MailChimp and Klaviyo both rank for the search term *"comparing Klaviyo and MailChimp"* — and use passive-aggressive copy to differentiate. Interestingly, Zapier often outranks both for these terms, capturing massive traffic despite the search being irrelevant to Zapier's core funnel.

> [!NOTE]
> **On Versus vs. Alternative-To pages:** In the early days of your business, focus on *Alternative-To* pages (riding a competitor's traffic). Only build *Versus* pages once people are actively searching for your brand name in comparisons.

---

### 🟢 Stage 5 — Most Aware

**Definition:** Your prospect is educated, close to buying, and ready to make a final decision. They may have already purchased and are now referring others.

**What they search for:** *"Buy Asics GT-2000" / "[Your Product] coupon code" / "[Your Product] features" / "[Your Product] security compliance"*

**Example (Realtor):** The realtor has evaluated all the options, narrowed it down, and is now looking for a final reason to commit — or a discount.

**Tactics:**
- **Feature Deep-Dives** — At this stage, break the *"benefits not features"* rule. Prospects need specifics. Show them exactly what they get.
- **Long-Form Demos** — Move from a 60-second homepage demo to a 30–45 minute walkthrough they can watch at their own pace.
- **Knowledge Base / Documentation** — A well-built knowledge base signals product maturity and gives buyers confidence.
- **Case Studies** — Now serving as final-push social proof rather than awareness-building.
- **Compliance & Security Pages** — *"SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR, Data Security"* — no one at Stage 1 cares about this. Stage 5 buyers care deeply.
- **White Papers** — For enterprise-level buyers, formal documentation can be a decisive factor.

**Real-World B2B Examples:**
- **Drip:** Feature-level pages that go beyond benefits to show exact product capabilities.
- **UserList:** Detailed integration library and specific feature documentation embedded in the marketing site.
- **HubSpot:** Salesforce vs. HubSpot pages, deep case studies, and a thoroughly documented product.
- **Sewell:** A dedicated security and compliance content page — a late-funnel asset that builds buyer confidence at exactly the right moment.

## 🔍 Pillar 1: How to Identify Which Stage a Prospect Is In

Identifying the stage of awareness is never 100% precise — aim for 75–80% accuracy. There are three primary methods.

### 1. Ask on Sales Calls

During discovery calls, ask qualifying questions that naturally reveal stage:

| Question | What It Reveals |
| :--- | :--- |
| *"When do you plan to make a purchase?"* | Near-purchase = Stage 4–5; Still researching = Stage 2–3 |
| *"Are you comparing us to other products?"* | Yes = Stage 4–5; No = Stage 2–3 |
| *"Which products have you looked at?"* | Named competitors = Stage 4; Named category = Stage 3 |
| *"Have you spoken to anyone else?"* | Yes = Stage 4–5; No = Stage 2–3 |

> [!TIP]
> Consider adding a **"Stage of Awareness"** field to your CRM alongside Budget and Timeline. Track where each prospect enters and whether they advance — this is high-signal data for refining your content strategy.

---

### 2. Keyword Intent Analysis

Keywords themselves signal which stage a searcher is in. Use this mapping as a guide:

| Keyword Intent Type | Stage | Example Keywords |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Informational** | Stage 2–3 | *"what is email marketing"*, *"how to send bulk email"* |
| **Commercial Investigation** | Stage 3–4 | *"best email marketing software"*, *"top 10 CRM tools"*, *"review of [product]"* |
| **Comparative** | Stage 4–5 | *"MailChimp vs Klaviyo"*, *"[Product A] alternative"*, *"compare [X] and [Y]"* |
| **Transactional** | Stage 5 | *"buy [product]"*, *"[product] coupon code"*, *"[product] discount"*, *"cheapest [product]"* |
| **Navigational** | Stage 4–5 | Direct brand/product name searches |

---

### 3. Use SEO & Intent Tools

Both major SEO platforms have built-in keyword intent classification:

- **SEMrush** — The keyword intent column tags results as: `C` (Commercial), `T` (Transactional), `N` (Navigational), `I` (Informational). Use this to sort and prioritize your keyword list by funnel stage.
- **SimilarWeb** — The intent column displays: Nav, Transact, Local, and Informational — a similar classification that maps directly to the five stages.

## ✍️ Pillar 2: Content Strategy by Stage

Use this table as your content planning and prioritization guide. **Start from the bottom (Stage 5) and work upward.**

| Stage | Awareness Level | Primary Content Types | Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stage 1** | Unaware | High-level YouTube videos, full-course content, outbound cold messaging | Expensive and slow — avoid spending heavily here early |
| **Stage 2** | Problem Aware | Templates, how-to guides, problem-specific blog posts | Give away what they need *right now* — no product pitch |
| **Stage 3** | Solution Aware | Category comparison content, G2/Capterra listings, forum presence | Be found where buyers are discovering the category |
| **Stage 4** | Product Aware | Versus pages, alternative-to pages, case studies, competitor monitoring | Piggyback on competitor brand for alternative-to pages early on |
| **Stage 5** | Most Aware | Feature pages, long-form demos, knowledge base, compliance pages, white papers | Break the "benefits only" rule — go deep on specifics |

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Why start at the bottom?** Stage 5 content closes sales AND does double duty as marketing material. It's the fastest path to revenue. Top-of-funnel content (Stage 1–2) may take months or years to convert.

## 📊 Pillar 3: Tools & Tactics for Execution

### Competitor & Mention Monitoring

Track when people mention your product or competitors by name in public forums — these are Stage 4 signals indicating active evaluation:

- **[F5Bot](https://f5bot.com)** — Monitors Reddit, Hacker News, and other public platforms for keyword mentions. Free tool. Sends email or Slack alerts.
- **[Syften](https://syften.com)** — Similar to F5Bot but reportedly crawls a broader set of sources including private Slack channels, Stack Overflow, Indie Hackers, and more.
- **Google Alerts** — Legacy option; significantly less effective in the current environment than the tools above.

> [!NOTE]
> When someone mentions your product by name in a forum, they are definitionally at Stage 4. Engaging with those conversations — even just to answer questions — can be a high-leverage activity.

---

### Keyword Research & Intent Tools

| Tool | Key Feature | Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SEMrush** | Intent classification (C/T/N/I) | Classify your keyword list by funnel stage |
| **SimilarWeb** | Intent column in keyword data | Cross-reference traffic intent against SEMrush |
| **WordStream** | Keyword discovery | Generate large keyword lists for your category |
| **Incognito Search** | SERP result analysis | Find which review platforms rank for your category terms |

---

### Review & Listing Platforms

Getting listed and active on these platforms is critical for Stage 3 visibility:

- **Capterra** — Often easiest to work with for SaaS advertisers; high buyer intent traffic.
- **G2** — Strong for larger SaaS categories; heavy review-driven ranking.
- **SourceForge** — Worth listing on as a supplemental platform.

> [!TIP]
> Search your category keyword in incognito (e.g., *"best e-signature software"*) to see which platform ranks #1 for your market. Prioritize your listing and ad spend on that platform first.

## 💡 Key Principles & Takeaways

1. **Start at Stage 5, work backwards.** The most-aware content closes deals *today* and provides sales literature that works whether or not you have a marketing engine.

2. **Content at the wrong stage doesn't convert — it just costs money.** General top-of-funnel content (the mistake from 2012) burns budget without driving revenue for early-stage companies.

3. **Attribution is imperfect — aim for 75–80%, not 100%.** Use UTM links, self-reported attribution fields on signup forms, and sales call questions to build a directionally accurate picture.

4. **Alternative-to pages before versus pages.** Early in your company's life, nobody is searching for your brand in comparisons. Piggyback on the category leaders' search volume instead.

5. **At Stage 5, break the "benefits not features" rule.** High-intent buyers need specifics — integration lists, feature tables, security documentation, and long demos. Give it to them.

6. **Keyword intent = stage signal.** Informational → Stage 2–3. Commercial/Comparative → Stage 3–4. Transactional → Stage 5. Use SEMrush's intent tags to sort your content backlog.

7. **Mention monitoring is Stage 4 prospecting.** When someone names your product in a Reddit thread or Hacker News post, they are already deep in the funnel. These conversations are worth your time.

8. **Content serves both marketing and sales.** Even if you do zero content marketing and only outbound sales, Stage 4–5 assets — case studies, versus pages, compliance pages, and long demos — directly accelerate deal closings.

# 🎬 The B2B SaaS YouTube Playbook

### Build a YouTube channel that generates real pipeline — a step-by-step execution guide for B2B SaaS companies starting from zero.

> **How this differs from the Framework:** The [framework](./framework.md) is the strategic reference — what to do and why. This playbook is the hands-on execution document — exact templates, fill-in-the-blank scripts, word-for-word examples, and the decisions you need to make at every step. Work through this phase by phase, in order, with a video in front of you.

---

## 🗺️ The 6-Phase Journey

| Phase | Name | Output When Done |
|-------|------|-----------------|
| **1** | Define Your Foundation | A single-sentence ideal viewer definition, one channel goal, one positioning statement |
| **2** | Research Your Topics | A spreadsheet of 30+ video ideas tagged by type and priority |
| **3** | Script Your Videos | 5 complete scripts that have passed the Script Review Checklist |
| **4** | Produce Your Videos | 5 recorded and edited videos ready to upload |
| **5** | Publish & Optimise | 5 live videos with titles, descriptions, thumbnails, and a complete channel page |
| **6** | Measure & Compound | A monthly review system running and a rolling 4-week content calendar |

## Phase 1 — 🧭 Define Your Foundation

> **Goal:** Get crystal clear on who you are making videos for, what problem you are solving, and what the channel is trying to achieve for the business — before you record a single second of video.

> ⚠️ **Skipping this phase is the single biggest mistake B2B SaaS companies make on YouTube.** They start recording before they know who they are talking to, and end up with a channel full of videos nobody searches for and nobody watches.

---

### Step 1.1 — Define Your Ideal Viewer

Your ideal viewer is not "everyone who might buy our product." It is one specific person at one specific stage of their journey. Answer every question below in writing before moving forward.

| Question | Example Answer |
|----------|---------------|
| What is their job title? | Head of Marketing at a B2B SaaS company |
| What is their biggest professional problem right now? | Not generating enough pipeline from organic channels |
| What do they search on YouTube when they have this problem? | "B2B content marketing strategy", "how to get leads from YouTube" |
| What stage are they at? | Just becoming aware of the problem, or actively looking for a solution? |
| What makes them trust you enough to book a call? | Seeing you help someone exactly like them get a specific result |

> 📌 **Rule:** Write your ideal viewer as a single sentence and put it somewhere visible.
>
> **Template:** *"My ideal viewer is a [job title] at a [company type] who is trying to [goal] but [obstacle]."*
>
> **Example:** *"My ideal viewer is a Head of Marketing at a bootstrapped B2B SaaS company who is trying to build an organic content engine but does not know where to start."*
>
> Every video you make should be made for this one person.

---

### Step 1.2 — Define Your Channel Goal

YouTube channels fail when they exist for vague reasons like "brand awareness." Your channel needs one specific business goal. Pick exactly **one** and stick with it for at least 6 months.

| Goal | What It Means in Practice |
|------|--------------------------|
| **Generate inbound leads** | Viewers watch your videos, trust you, and book a discovery call |
| **Shorten the sales cycle** | Prospects watch your videos before or after a sales call to build confidence |
| **Drive trial or product signups** | Videos introduce the product and lead viewers to a free trial |
| **Build founder/brand authority** | Position the founder or company as the go-to expert in the niche |

> 💡 Your goal determines everything — the types of videos you make, the calls-to-action you use, and how you measure success. Choose one and commit.

---

### Step 1.3 — Write Your Channel Positioning Statement

Your channel needs a clear, specific angle. Fill in this sentence exactly:

```
"This channel is for [ideal viewer]
who want to [desired outcome]
without [common frustration or obstacle]."
```

**Example:**

> *"This channel is for B2B SaaS founders who want to build a YouTube audience that generates inbound leads without spending money on ads or hiring a full content team."*

Put this in your channel description verbatim.

- [ ] One primary channel goal selected

- [ ] Everyone on the team agrees on the above before any video is made

## Phase 2 — 🔍 Research Your Topics

> **Goal:** Build a list of 30+ video ideas that your ideal viewer is actively searching for — so that your videos get found, not just made.

---

### Step 2.1 — The Three Types of B2B YouTube Topics

Not all video topics serve the same purpose. You need a mix of all three. Most beginners only make one type — don't make that mistake.

| Type | Purpose | Examples |
|------|---------|---------|
| 🔍 **Search-driven** | Get found by buyers actively researching a problem or solution | "How to build a B2B content strategy", "Best CRM for SaaS startups" |
| 💡 **Authority** | Establish you as the go-to expert in your niche | "Why most B2B SaaS companies fail at content marketing", "The strategy that took us from 0 to $1M ARR" |
| 🛠️ **Product-led** | Show your product solving a specific problem in action | "How [Company Name] automates your sales pipeline", "Tutorial: Setting up your first campaign in [Product]" |

> 📌 **Rule for beginners:** Start with search-driven topics. They are the fastest path to getting your first views from people who are actually looking for what you offer.

---

### Step 2.2 — Four Methods to Find What Your Buyers Are Searching For

Use at least two of these methods before building your topic list.

**Method 1 — YouTube Search Autocomplete** *(Free, start here)*

Go to YouTube. Start typing a problem your ideal viewer has. Do *not* press Enter. Write down every autocomplete suggestion. These are real things people are searching for right now.

Try these starting phrases:
- `how to [problem your product solves]`
- `[your niche] strategy`
- `best [category your product fits in]`
- `[your niche] for SaaS`

**Method 2 — Competitor Channel Research** *(Free)*

Find 3–5 YouTube channels your ideal viewer already watches. Sort their videos by "Most Popular." High-view videos on competitor channels are proven topics — people are already searching for them. Make your own, better version.

**Method 3 — Reddit & Online Communities** *(Free)*

Go to the subreddits, Slack communities, or forums where your ideal viewer spends time. Look for the questions asked most often. Every repeated question is a video topic. Use their exact words in your video title — not your internal jargon.

**Method 4 — Your Own Sales Calls** *(Free, most valuable)*

The questions prospects ask most often before they buy are your best video topics. Ask your sales team: *"What are the top 5 questions every prospect asks before they buy?"* These are real buyers at the exact decision moment.

---

### Step 2.3 — Build Your Topic Spreadsheet

Create a spreadsheet and fill in at least 30 rows before moving to Phase 3. Don't worry about perfect titles yet — just capture the idea.

| # | Video Topic / Working Title | Type | Search Term | Priority |
|---|----------------------------|------|-------------|----------|
| 1 | How to build a B2B content strategy from scratch | Search | b2b content strategy | High |
| 2 | Why we stopped running paid ads | Authority | stop paid ads saas | Medium |
| 3 | Setting up your first campaign in [Product] | Product | [product] tutorial | Low |
| … | Continue until you have 30+ rows | | | |

---

- [ ] You understand the three types of B2B YouTube topics
- [ ] You have used at least 2 of the 4 research methods above
- [ ] You have a list of at least 30 video topics in a spreadsheet
- [ ] Each topic is tagged by type (search-driven, authority, or product-led)
- [ ] You have identified your top 10 priority topics to produce first

## Phase 3 — ✍️ Script Your Videos

> **Goal:** Write a script for every video using a repeatable structure that hooks the viewer in the first 30 seconds and leads them toward a clear action by the end.

> 💡 A well-scripted video recorded on a phone will outperform a poorly scripted video shot in a professional studio. Every time. The script is the most important part of production.

---

### Step 3.1 — The 5-Part B2B SaaS Video Structure

Every video you make should follow this exact five-part structure. Do not skip any part.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  PART 1 — THE HOOK  (0:00 – 0:30)                           │
│  Make the viewer stop scrolling and keep watching.          │
│  Name their exact problem.                                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PART 2 — THE PROMISE  (0:30 – 1:00)                        │
│  Tell them exactly what they will learn or get              │
│  by watching the entire video.                              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PART 3 — THE CONTENT  (Middle)                             │
│  Deliver the value you promised. No fluff.                  │
│  3–5 main points with examples.                             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PART 4 — THE BRIDGE  (Before CTA)                          │
│  Connect what they just learned to your product or next     │
│  step — without making it feel like an ad.                  │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PART 5 — THE CALL TO ACTION  (Last 0:30)                   │
│  Tell them exactly what to do next.                         │
│  One action only — never two or three.                      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

### Step 3.2 — Write Your Hook

The hook is the most important part of the script. If you lose the viewer in the first 30 seconds, nothing else matters. Pick one of these three hook types for every video.

**Hook Type 1 — Problem Hook** *(Name the pain directly)*

> *"If you have been posting on LinkedIn for months and still not getting inbound leads, this video is going to show you exactly why — and how to fix it."*

**Hook Type 2 — Result Hook** *(Lead with the outcome)*

> *"In the last 6 months, we went from zero to 40 inbound demo requests a month using only YouTube. Here is the exact system we used."*

**Hook Type 3 — Mistake Hook** *(Call out a common error)*

> *"Most B2B SaaS companies are building their YouTube channel completely backwards. Here is what I mean — and what to do instead."*

> 🚫 **Never** start a video with "Hey guys, welcome back to the channel" or your company introduction. Start with the viewer's problem. They clicked because of their problem, not because of you.

---

### Step 3.3 — The Full Script Template

Copy this template for every video. Fill in the brackets. Do not skip any section.

```
VIDEO TITLE:    [Working title]
TARGET VIEWER:  [Who is this video for?]
SEARCH TERM:    [What will they type to find this?]
VIDEO GOAL:     [What should the viewer do after watching?]

──────────────────────────────────────────────

HOOK  (0:00 – 0:30)
Start with the problem, result, or mistake. No introductions.

[Write your hook here]

──────────────────────────────────────────────

PROMISE  (0:30 – 1:00)
Tell them: "In this video, you will learn / I will show you /
By the end of this video you will have..."

[Write your promise here]

──────────────────────────────────────────────

CONTENT  (1:00 – end of main body)

Point 1: [Subheading]
  — What you will say
  — Example or proof

Point 2: [Subheading]
  — What you will say
  — Example or proof

Point 3: [Subheading]
  — What you will say
  — Example or proof

[Add or remove points. Aim for 3–5 per video.]

──────────────────────────────────────────────

BRIDGE  (Before CTA)
Connect the content back to your product naturally.
e.g. "This is exactly the problem [Product] was built to solve..."
or "If you want to skip all this manual work, we have a free resource..."

[Write your bridge here]

──────────────────────────────────────────────

CALL TO ACTION  (Last 30 seconds)
One action only. Examples:
  — "Download the free template in the description"
  — "Book a free strategy call at [link]"
  — "Try [Product] free for 14 days — link below"

[Write your CTA here]

──────────────────────────────────────────────

ESTIMATED LENGTH: [X] minutes
```

---

### Step 3.4 — Script Review Checklist

Run every script through this check before you record. If you answer "no" to any question, fix it before pressing record.

- [ ] Does the hook start with the viewer's problem — not a greeting or company intro?
- [ ] Does the promise tell the viewer exactly what they'll get by watching the whole video?
- [ ] Is every point in the content section backed by an example, a number, or a real case?
- [ ] Is there zero fluff — no filler sentences that do not add value?
- [ ] Does the bridge connect naturally to the product without feeling like an ad?
- [ ] Is there exactly one call to action — not two or three?
- [ ] Would your ideal viewer (defined in Phase 1) watch this entire video?

---

### ✅ Phase 3 Checklist

- [ ] You understand the five-part B2B SaaS video structure
- [ ] You have chosen a hook type for each video
- [ ] You have scripted your first 5 videos using the template above
- [ ] Every script has passed the Script Review Checklist

## Phase 4 — 🎥 Produce Your Videos

> **Goal:** Record and edit your first 5 videos to a standard that builds trust with a B2B buyer — without over-investing in equipment before you've validated your content.

> ⚠️ The most common mistake at this stage is **waiting for a perfect setup** before starting. You do not need a studio, DSLR camera, or professional editor to make your first videos.

---

### Step 4.1 — The Minimum Viable Setup

| Element | Minimum Standard | What to Use |
|---------|-----------------|-------------|
| 🎙️ **Audio** | Clear, no echo, no background noise | USB microphone (Blue Yeti, Rode NT-USB) or a lapel mic. **Most important investment.** Bad audio kills trust faster than bad video. |
| 💡 **Lighting** | Face is well-lit and not shadowy | Sit facing a window for natural light, or buy a simple ring light (under ₹2,000). |
| 📷 **Camera** | Sharp, stable, no blurry footage | Your phone on a tripod is completely fine. A 1080p webcam also works. |
| 🖥️ **Background** | Clean and uncluttered | A plain wall, bookshelf, or simple virtual background. Avoid messy or distracting backgrounds. |
| ✂️ **Editing** | Cuts, captions, title cards | DaVinci Resolve (free), CapCut (free), or iMovie (free on Mac). You do not need Premiere Pro to start. |

---

### Step 4.2 — Choose the Right Format for Each Video

| Format | Best For | How to Record |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| 🎤 **Talking Head** | Opinion, authority, founder-led content | Face to camera, one location, scripted or semi-scripted |
| 🖥️ **Screen Recording** | Tutorials, product walkthroughs, how-to content | Loom (free) or OBS (free) — record your screen while talking through what you are doing |
| 📊 **Slides + Voiceover** | Frameworks, strategy breakdowns, data-heavy content | Record your screen while presenting a slide deck. Simple and fast to produce. |
| 🎙️ **Interview** | Case studies, customer stories, expert conversations | Record via Riverside.fm or Zoom. Edit into a structured video. |

> ✅ **Tip for beginners:** Start with screen recordings or slides + voiceover. These are the easiest to produce, require zero on-camera confidence, and work extremely well for B2B tutorial and strategy content.

---

### Step 4.3 — Pre-Recording Checklist

Run this before every recording session.

- [ ] Phone is on Do Not Disturb
- [ ] All desktop notifications are turned off
- [ ] Room is as quiet as possible — no AC, fan, or street noise if avoidable
- [ ] 30-second test recording done and played back to check audio and lighting
- [ ] Script or key talking points are visible but not being read word-for-word
- [ ] Glass of water nearby

---

### Step 4.4 — Editing Principles for B2B SaaS Videos

B2B buyers do not care about flashy transitions — they care about getting the information efficiently.

| Principle | What It Means in Practice |
|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Cut every pause and filler** | Remove all "ums," "uhs," long pauses, and restarts. This alone makes any video feel more professional. |
| **Always add captions** | 85% of videos are watched without sound. Always add captions. Use CapCut or Rev.com. |
| **Use chapter markers** | Add timestamps in the description so viewers can jump to what they need. Signals to YouTube that your video is well-structured. |
| **Add a title card at the start** | A simple text overlay showing the video title helps set context immediately. |
| **Keep it as short as it needs to be** | A B2B SaaS video should be exactly as long as it needs to be and no longer. If a point can be made in 5 minutes, do not pad it to 10. |

---

### ✅ Phase 4 Checklist

- [ ] Minimum viable setup is in place (audio, lighting, camera, background)
- [ ] You have chosen the right format for each of your first 5 videos
- [ ] All 5 videos are recorded
- [ ] All 5 videos are edited — pauses cut, captions added, chapter markers planned
- [ ] You have watched each video back and are satisfied it represents you professionally

## Phase 5 — 🚀 Publish & Optimise

> **Goal:** Upload your videos in a way that maximises the chance of them being found by your ideal viewer — through YouTube search, suggested videos, and Google search.

---

### Step 5.1 — Write a Title That Gets Clicked

Your title has two jobs: get found in search, and get clicked when found. A title that only does one of these will underperform.

**The formula:**

```
[Specific Outcome or Problem] + [For Whom or In What Context]
```

**Weak vs. strong title examples:**

| ❌ Weak Title | ✅ Strong Title |
|--------------|---------------|
| YouTube Strategy | How to Build a B2B SaaS YouTube Channel That Generates Inbound Leads |
| Content Marketing Tips | The B2B Content Strategy That Took Us From 0 to 40 Demos a Month |
| How We Use LinkedIn | Why We Stopped Paid Ads and Built a YouTube Channel Instead |
| SaaS Marketing | YouTube SEO for B2B SaaS: How to Rank for the Terms Your Buyers Search |

**Title rules:**
- Keep it under 60 characters so it doesn't get cut off in search results
- Put the most important keyword near the front
- Use a number when possible (e.g. "5 Steps", "3 Mistakes")
- Never use clickbait — B2B buyers will not forgive it

---

### Step 5.2 — Write a Description That Works

The description is not just a summary — it's an SEO asset and a conversion tool. Use this structure for every video.

```
[First 2–3 sentences: Repeat the core promise of the video.
These lines show up in search results — make them count.]

─────────────────────────────────────

🔗 RESOURCES MENTIONED
— [Link 1 — free resource, template, or tool mentioned]
— [Link 2]

─────────────────────────────────────

📅 CHAPTERS
0:00 — Introduction
[Timestamp] — [Chapter Title]
[Timestamp] — [Chapter Title]
[Timestamp] — [Chapter Title]

─────────────────────────────────────

🚀 READY TO [DESIRED OUTCOME]?
[Your CTA — book a call, try the product, download a resource]
[Link]

─────────────────────────────────────

[3–5 relevant hashtags at the very bottom]
```

---

### Step 5.3 — Design a Thumbnail That Stops the Scroll

| Principle | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Use your face if possible** | People buy from people. A face on a thumbnail builds trust in B2B faster than a graphic. |
| **Use large, readable text** | Your thumbnail will be the size of a postage stamp on mobile. If the text isn't readable at that size, it's too small. |
| **Use 2–3 colours maximum** | A busy thumbnail is an ignored thumbnail. Pick a consistent colour palette and stick with it across all videos. |
| **Show the outcome, not the topic** | Instead of "Content Strategy," show "0 → 40 Demos/Month" — the result, not the subject. |
| **Be consistent across all videos** | Consistent style builds brand recognition. Viewers will recognise your videos in their feed before they read the title. |

> 🎨 Design your thumbnails in **Canva** — the free tier is enough to start. Create one template and reuse it for every video.

---

### Step 5.4 — Channel Page Setup Checklist

Do this before publishing your first video.

- [ ] **Channel name** — your company name or your own name if founder-led
- [ ] **Channel description** — one clear sentence on who the channel is for and what they will get. Use your Phase 1 positioning statement.
- [ ] **Channel banner** — shows your name/company and the value of the channel. Design in Canva.
- [ ] **Profile picture** — your face (founder-led) or your logo. A real photo will almost always outperform a logo for B2B trust-building.
- [ ] **Channel trailer** — 60–90 second video: who the channel is for, what they'll get, why they should subscribe.
- [ ] **Links** — add your website, booking link, and free resources to the channel links section.

---

### ✅ Phase 5 Checklist

- [ ] Every video has a title following the formula above
- [ ] Every video has a structured description with chapters, links, and a CTA
- [ ] Every video has a thumbnail designed in a consistent style
- [ ] Channel page is fully set up — name, description, banner, profile photo, trailer, links
- [ ] First 5 videos are published as public — not unlisted

## Phase 6 — 📈 Measure & Compound

> **Goal:** Build a simple monthly review system that tells you what is working, what is not, and what to do more of — so the channel compounds over time instead of plateauing.

---

### Step 6.1 — The Only Metrics That Matter for B2B SaaS YouTube

Ignore vanity metrics like total views and subscriber count. Track these five instead.

| Metric | What It Tells You | Where to Find It | Target |
|--------|------------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** | How many people click your video when they see it in search or suggestions | YouTube Studio → Analytics → Reach | 4–8% for B2B |
| **Average View Duration (AVD)** | How long people watch before leaving — the single best signal of content quality | YouTube Studio → Engagement | 40%+ of total length |
| **Watch Time** | Total minutes watched — YouTube's main ranking signal | YouTube Studio → Overview | Growing month over month |
| **Traffic Source: YouTube Search** | How much traffic comes from people actively searching | YouTube Studio → Reach → Traffic Sources | Should be your largest source |
| **Leads / Demos Booked from YouTube** ⭐ | The actual business outcome — the number your CEO will ask about | UTM links in descriptions, tracked in your CRM | Set your own baseline in Month 1 |

---

### Step 6.2 — The Monthly Review Process

Do this on the same day every month. It takes 30–45 minutes. **Block it in your calendar now.**

**Step 1 — Check your top 3 videos by watch time this month**

What topic? What type (search/authority/product)? What did the title and thumbnail look like?
→ Make more videos like these.

**Step 2 — Check your bottom 3 videos by watch time this month**

What did they have in common? Topic, title, thumbnail, or the content itself?
→ Stop making videos like these, or fix the title/thumbnail and retest.

**Step 3 — Check CTR on every video**

- CTR below 3%: try a new title and thumbnail
- CTR above 6%: study what made it work and replicate the pattern

**Step 4 — Check Average View Duration on every video**

- Drop off before 30%: your hook is not working — rewrite it for future videos
- Past 60%: content is strong — this format is resonating

**Step 5 — Check leads/demos attributed to YouTube**

Are UTM links tracking correctly? How many leads came from YouTube this month? Which videos are driving the most CTA clicks?

**Step 6 — Plan next month's content**

Take your Phase 2 topic list. Prioritise based on what the data is telling you. Aim to publish at minimum 2 videos per month (4 is better; daily is unnecessary for B2B).

---

### Step 6.3 — The Content Compounding Timeline

B2B SaaS YouTube is a long-term compounding channel, not a short-term traffic channel. Here is what realistic growth looks like:

| Month | What to Expect | What to Focus On |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|
| 1–2 | Very few views. Almost no leads. This is completely normal. | Publish consistently. Improve scripting with each video. |
| 3–4 | Some videos start getting traction in search. A few leads come in. | Double down on topics and formats getting traction. |
| 5–6 | Clear patterns emerge. Certain video types consistently perform. | Systematise what is working. Build a repeatable schedule. |
| 7–12 | Compounding kicks in. Older videos keep generating views. Leads become more consistent. | Keep publishing. Build playlists. Link videos to each other. |
| 12+ | The channel is an asset. It generates leads without you actively promoting each video. | Expand into new topic clusters and formats. |

> 📌 **The key rule:** Do not judge the channel in month 1, 2, or 3. Judge it at month 6. Almost every B2B SaaS channel that succeeded looked like it was failing in its first 90 days.

---

### ✅ Phase 6 Checklist

- [ ] UTM links are set up in every video description so leads can be tracked to YouTube
- [ ] YouTube Studio analytics is being checked at minimum once a month
- [ ] Monthly review process is scheduled as a recurring calendar event
- [ ] A content calendar for the next 4 weeks always exists
- [ ] You are publishing at minimum 2 videos per month consistently

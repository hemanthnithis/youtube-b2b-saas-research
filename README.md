## 📌 Overview

This repository is my personal research playbook for understanding how B2B SaaS companies can use YouTube as a serious content and growth channel. I built it by going deep into the work of creators, founders, and strategists who are actually doing this — not talking about it in theory, but running channels, publishing consistently, and tying their content directly to business outcomes like leads, pipeline, and acquisitions.

The research is split across two groups: **YouTube creators** who practice YouTube content strategy directly on their own channels, and **LinkedIn authors** whose frameworks on B2B content, SEO, and audience-building add the strategic depth needed to make a YouTube content playbook actually work in a SaaS context.

---

## 💡 Why I Chose This Topic

I chose YouTube content strategy for B2B SaaS because it sits at the intersection of two things I find genuinely underexplored: the way B2B companies build trust with buyers over time, and the way video as a medium is rapidly becoming the default format for how people research, learn, and make decisions — including in professional and enterprise contexts.

Most B2B SaaS companies treat YouTube as an afterthought. They either repurpose blog posts into talking-head videos with no real strategy behind them, or they ignore the channel entirely in favour of LinkedIn and paid ads. But a growing number of high-performing SaaS companies are proving that YouTube, done with intention, can be one of the most powerful compounding channels a B2B business has — driving organic discovery, building brand authority, and shortening sales cycles because prospects already trust you before they ever book a call.

That gap between how most B2B SaaS companies use YouTube and how the best ones use it is what drew me to this topic. I wanted to understand what separates a YouTube channel that generates real pipeline from one that just generates views — and to build a research-backed playbook that answers that question clearly.

This is also a topic that is maturing fast. The rise of AI-driven search, the shift in how B2B buyers self-educate before talking to sales, and the growing importance of owned audiences over rented ones (like paid ads) all make YouTube strategy increasingly critical for B2B SaaS companies to get right. Researching it now, while the playbook is still being written by practitioners in real time, felt like exactly the right moment.

---

## 🚀 Why I Started This Research

Most YouTube advice is built for consumer creators — views, subscribers, ad revenue. Almost none of it is built for B2B SaaS founders or marketers who need YouTube to drive discovery, trust, and pipeline from a very specific kind of buyer.

I wanted to find the people who have figured this out — either by building a YouTube channel that grows their own SaaS, or by documenting exactly how the content-to-revenue connection works inside a B2B company. This repository is where I collect, organise, and build on what I learn from them.

---

## 📝 A Note on Content Scope

Not every piece of content I have researched here is exclusively about YouTube. Some creators focus on broader content marketing, B2B SEO, messaging strategy, or audience-building across multiple channels. I have deliberately included these voices because the principles they teach — how to structure content for a specific buyer, how to write hooks that convert, how to build a content system that compounds over time — apply directly and entirely to YouTube content production.

> **YouTube is not a separate discipline from content marketing. It is content marketing expressed through video.** The frameworks these creators teach on scripting, positioning, editorial planning, and audience trust translate one-to-one into how a B2B SaaS company should approach its YouTube channel. That is why I chose them.

## 🎯 Why I Chose These Creators

Selecting the right sources was the most important decision in this research. There is no shortage of content marketing advice on the internet — the challenge is finding voices whose work is grounded in real outcomes, not just good-sounding frameworks.

Every creator and author in this playbook was selected against the same four criteria:

| Criteria | What It Means |
|---|---|
| 🔨 **Practitioners First** | Each person drives measurable business results — leads, sales, acquisitions, pipeline — or has documented with full transparency exactly how they built it. No theorists. |
| 🎯 **B2B Specificity** | Every person on this list speaks directly to the realities of B2B SaaS — longer sales cycles, multiple stakeholders, high-intent buyer behaviour — even when their content touches broader topics. |
| 📹 **Transferability to YouTube** | Even where a creator's primary medium is LinkedIn or a podcast, their frameworks on messaging, audience-building, and content structure apply without translation to how a B2B SaaS company scripts, produces, and distributes YouTube content. |
| 🔀 **Diversity of Angle** | Building a B2B SaaS YouTube channel requires thinking across multiple disciplines at once: search, scripting, editorial planning, production systems, audience trust, and pipeline attribution. These eleven creators cover all of those angles together. |
